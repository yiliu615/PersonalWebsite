Biostatistics • Clinical Collaboration • Methodological Innovation

Yi Liu, Ph.D. Candidate at Emory University

I develop practical, high-impact statistical methods for modern biomedical data, with a focus on survival and longitudinal outcomes, latent-variable models, and quantile regression for recurrent event processes.

My work is driven by a clear mission: transforming complex data into reliable scientific evidence that helps clinicians make better decisions for patients. I bring an energetic and collaborative style to every project, bridging statistical rigor with real-world clinical needs.

Research Vision

Building next-generation analytical tools that reveal heterogeneity, improve interpretability, and accelerate evidence generation in oncology and translational health research.

Current Focus

Quantile-based methods for recurrent episodes, flexible latent-variable frameworks for multivariate outcomes, and learning-based phenotyping in electronic health records.

Open to Collaboration

I actively collaborate across biostatistics, pathology, and endocrine research teams and welcome opportunities for methodological and applied partnerships.
